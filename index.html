Data skills are not just for those employees with "data" in their job title. All employees at all levels need to be data literate. Improved data literacy is needed to make better sense of the huge volumes of data available to organisations and consequently to make better data-driven business decisions to enable business success.
Where are you on the data literacy spectrum?

AWARE...ACCESSS...ENGAGE....MANAGE...COMMUNICATE...ETHICAL USE...
Data literacy defined (mouse over here) Data literacy is the ability to collect, process, and extract meaning from data, and in doing so, convert data to actionable insights.

DATA AWARE/ACCESS
The early stages of data literacy involve understanding the role of data and then how identify and use data appropriately. Questions to consider: What data are you collecting? What data should you be collecting? How are you planning to use this data? What business problems are you looking to resolve?

ENGAGE/MANAGE
Subsequent stages of data literacy involve evaluating, analysing and interpreting data and findings for the purpose of decision making. Discover the hidden patterns in your data. Make comparisons between groups. Data visualisation is essential to translate data into information. Questions to consider: What comparisons are useful to solve the business problem? What are we expecting form the data? What are the relationships in our data? What does the broader evidence tell us in terms of benchmarking and research evidence?

COMMUNICATE
This stage of data literacy requires synthesising, visualising and representing the data. Here it is important to ensure the data and findings are linked with business decisions and actions. Questions to consider: How are the findings linked to business deicisions? How are insights linked with the business problem? What are the blockers for change? What would the change look like? How can we measure/determine whether the change is successful? More data...
